Yamamoto Nasty In Another Dodger Victory

Yoshinobu Yamamoto, the Dodgers star pitcher, brings his nastiest stuff in a win against the Mets on Thursday. He recorded a postseason high chase percentage, thanks to his dominant fastball and deceptive offspeed.

After a blazing hot Mark Vientos hit a solo home run, Yamamoto doubled down and tossed 4 strong innings, and was brought out of the game for Evan Phillips in the 5th.

Yamamoto was backed by plenty of run support, a pattern now for the Dodgers, as they scored 10 runs in the contest. Ohtani and Betts both had home runs, and RBIs from Kike Hernandez and Tommy Edman.
