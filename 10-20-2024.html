Treinen Gets a Six-Out Save to Clinch World Series

The Dodgers, the team expected to win the World Series, have managed to live another day, on the backs of the strong bullpen. The Dodgers had lost most of their starting pitching, with just three arms left: Yoshinobu Yamamoto, Jack Flaherty, and Walker Buehler.

They pushed through this lack of pitching however, and scored double digit runs in Game 6, thanks to Tommy Edman, Mookie Betts, and Will Smith.

Blake Treinen the closer at the top of manager Dave Roberts' trust tree, came in to pitch in the top of the eigth, and came out again the top of the ninth to secure the victory. This is a sight we have seen before, as Treinen was also the one to close out the NLDS. So the question remains: Will Treinen get his chance to do it again?

The nasty reliever pitched two strong innings, with a massive chase rate, strikeout rate, and soft contact rate. His dominance earned him the season high 306 DL+! He was 206% better than the average pitcher, and has been dominant this whole postseason. Will he continue on this path?
