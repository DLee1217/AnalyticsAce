Walker Buehler Navigates Through Strong Start, Dodgers Blow Out Mets

Walker Buehler, once the leader of a promising Dodgers rotation, had undergone his second Tommy John surgery and had missed the entire 2023 season rehabbing. When he returned, Buehler struggled mightily, posting a 5.39 ERA and losing more games than he won.

However, the goal of baseball is to win in October, and Buehler was able to do that on Wednesday with a strong 4 inning start against the Mets, surrendering 0 runs on 3 hits. He faced off against Luis Severino, the Mets power pitcher, who gave up two unearned run on his way to an NLCS loss.

Buehler's first start in the postseason, game 3 of the NLDS, raised doubts that he would be able to perform on the highest stage, as he gave up 6 runs in a 6 - 5 loss against the Padres. However, he rebounded in his next start, providing just enough length for the Dodgers bullpen to take over and shut the Mets down.

It appears that Buehler is done for the rest of the NLCS, and if the Dodgers advance to the World Series, he is sure to pitch in either game 2 or game 3.

Recently, the DL stat was changed to DL+, with 100 being league average and the pitcher would be DL - 100 % better than the average pitcher. In his latest start, Buehler achieved a 116 DL+, meaning he was 16% better than the average pitcher. He edged out Luis Severino, who recorded a 107 DL+. Buehler was unable to induce soft contact, but had a good strikeout percentage, which brought him over Severino.
