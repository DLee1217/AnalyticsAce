Yamamoto Delivers in World Series Debut

Yoshinobu Yamamoto, the star pitcher from Japan, has proved once again his dominance on the big stage, turning in a magnificent start for the Dodgers in the pivotal Game 2.

Yamamoto has proved that he is clutch in the Japan Series and the World Baseball Classic, where he shined. Similarly, Yamamoto's start on Saturday was also amazing, as he pitched into the 7th inning for the first time since his June start against the Yankees.

Although Yamamoto gave up a home run and did not have good peripherals, his traditional counting stats were great enough to propel him to a 146 DL+, way better than his counterpart, Carlos Rodon.

The Dodgers now have a two game advantage going into Yankee Stadium, as another homer from Tommy Edman and back to back home runs from Teoscar Hernandez and Freddie Freeman.
