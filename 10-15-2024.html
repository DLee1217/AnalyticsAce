Gerrit Cole Escapes Jam En Route to a Victory

Despite a bases-loaded jam, Gerrit Cole managed to escape damage, letting the Yankees to run away with the win. Cole's start was cut short due to traffic on the bases, but overall it was an above average start for Cole.

Cole's DL of 107 for this game is above average, but his high walk rate and inability to strike people out hurt him, but the low hard contact rate and low isolated power brought him up to a good start.

Aaron Judge, the Yankees' superstar, was yet to break out, but he managed to hit a 3-run home run, a great sign for the Yankees offense, who already has the likes of Juan Soto, Jazz Chisolm, and Gleyber Torres, looks to be unstoppable this postseason.

Historically, teams that have gone up 2-0 have won 80% of the time. It seems the Yankees are going to steamroll to the World Series, where they will meet either the Mets or the Dodgers.
